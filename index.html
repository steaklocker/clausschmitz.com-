01 · The Commercial Problem

European premium equipment manufacturers consistently underperform their North American commercial potential.

Not because the product isn't good enough — it usually is. Because the gap between what the machine is capable of and what the customer actually realizes from it is never closed.

The telemetry goes unread. The workflow transformation doesn't happen. The ROI conversation never reaches the ownership level where capital decisions actually get made. The machine gets evaluated on its sticker price rather than its operational impact, and the next sales cycle starts from zero.

Claus Schmitz has occupied all three of those chairs — as a hotel ownership-group executive approving capital, as a chef running the kitchen the machine goes into, and as a connected-product founder who built the telemetry layer himself. Not sequentially — simultaneously, across three continents.

A premium product not converting at the rate its quality deserves
Telemetry and connected-product capability generating no commercial value
A gap between what engineering builds and what the customer experiences
North American market entry requiring European cultural and linguistic credibility
Quality or warranty issues damaging margin and customer confidence
Leadership that can build and sell the machine but not close the institutional conversation
